What Is AI Search Optimization (GEO) and Why Tradespeople Should Care

ChatGPT and Perplexity are changing how customers find local services. Here's what tradespeople need to know about Generative Engine Optimization — and how to get ahead of it.

Something shifted in 2024 that most small business owners haven't noticed yet. When someone types "best electrician near Prunedale" or "home inspector in Santa Cruz" into Google, they might not see ten blue links anymore. They see an AI-generated answer at the top — built from whatever Google, ChatGPT, or Perplexity decides is authoritative.

That answer either includes your business or it doesn't.

This is Generative Engine Optimization — GEO for short — and it's the next frontier of local SEO. Most tradespeople have never heard of it. That's your opportunity.

How AI Search Actually Works

Traditional search engines crawl the web, index it, and serve you a ranked list. AI search engines do something different: they synthesize information from multiple sources and generate a direct answer.

When someone asks ChatGPT "Who's a good electrician in Salinas, CA?", the model doesn't just list websites. It pulls from business directories, review sites, local citations, structured data, and websites it has previously crawled — and it builds a recommendation on the fly.

The businesses that get mentioned are the ones whose information is:

  • Clear, consistent, and well-structured across the web
  • Backed by legitimate reviews and citations
  • Presented in a format AI systems can easily parse (think schema markup, clear service descriptions, FAQ sections)
  • Tied to a geographic area through location-specific content

If your site was built in 2018, has no schema, and your business name is spelled three different ways across Yelp, Google, and your website — you won't show up. The AI won't trust you.

Why Tradespeople Are Especially Vulnerable

Most trade businesses — electricians, plumbers, inspectors, roofers, HVAC contractors — rely entirely on Google Maps and word of mouth. Their websites are afterthoughts. Their citations are inconsistent. They have no schema markup. They've never heard the term "structured data."

Meanwhile, their competitors in 2026 are not other plumbers. They're aggregator sites like Angi, Thumbtack, and HomeAdvisor — which have enormous SEO authority and show up in AI answers constantly because they're well-structured, well-cited, and have thousands of reviews.

The window to get ahead of this is right now, before AI search becomes the default for local queries. Every month you wait, the aggregators extend their lead.

What GEO Actually Looks Like for a Trade Business

Here's what Ember SEO does when we optimize a trade business for AI search:

1. Schema Markup
We add structured data to your site that tells AI systems exactly who you are, what you do, where you work, and how to contact you. LocalBusiness schema, Service schema, FAQPage schema — all of it. This is the language AI reads.

2. Citation Consistency
Your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) needs to be identical across every directory. Google Business Profile, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Facebook — if they don't match, AI systems see conflicting data and deprioritize you.

3. City & Service Pages
AI search is hyper-local. A page that says "Electrician in Prunedale, CA" with your license number, a service description, and local context will get picked up by AI answers for Prunedale queries. One page per city, done right, can generate leads for years.

4. FAQ Content
AI loves answering questions. If your site has a well-structured FAQ that answers the questions customers actually ask ("How much does a home inspection cost in Santa Cruz?"), AI search engines are much more likely to quote your content — and mention your business.

5. Google Business Profile Optimization
Your GBP is often the primary source AI systems pull from for local business recommendations. A complete, actively managed profile with real reviews, photos, and a complete services list is non-negotiable.

The Bottom Line

The shift to AI search isn't coming. It's here. And most tradespeople are completely unprepared for it.

The good news: the bar is low. If you get this right before your competitors do, you own that territory. AI recommendations are sticky — once a model associates your business with quality work in your service area, that trust compounds over time.
